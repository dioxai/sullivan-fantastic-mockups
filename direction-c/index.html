The first thing I'll tell you is that I don't do gigs. I host evenings. There's a difference, and you'll feel it the moment the room settles and the first phrase lands.

For the last decade I've been the voice — and quietly, the producer — behind some of the more particular rooms in the country. Galas where the guest list is half the headline. The Salon

Three kinds of evening.

No packages. No tiers. Just three rooms I know well — and the instincts to design the night you actually want.

— i.

Private Evenings

Milestone birthdays in private homes. Anniversaries on the water. Black-tie dinners with thirty of the right people. The kind of night where the music isn't background — it's the reason everyone stays past midnight. I bring the voice, the band, and the read of the room.

Corporate & Galas

Who I Host

The right room.

Hosts who plan the night themselves. Families on their third milestone. Foundations whose honorees still answer their own emails. Couples whose taste is quieter than their budget. Planners who'd rather not babysit the talent.

If your last great evening is still being talked about by the people who were there — we're going to get along.

How It Works

Three conversations. One night.

— Step one

We talk.

A real call — not a form-reply. Twenty minutes, on the phone, with me. I'll ask about the room, the guests, what's actually being celebrated, and what would make the night feel like yours.

— Step two

We design the evening.

A short written treatment: format, set list, key beats, who I'm playing with, when the room rises. Refined with you until it reads exactly the way the night should feel.

— Step three

I host.

I arrive early. I shake the right hands. I read the room. I sing. And when the night ends, your guests leave already telling the story of it — which is, in the end, the whole job.

Concierge

Tell me about the night.

A few lines is plenty. I read every note myself, and I reply within forty-eight hours — usually less.
