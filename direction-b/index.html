An Introduction · Editor's Letter

There are entertainers who fill a calendar, and there are those who fill a room. Chris Sullivan is the latter — and increasingly, the only name the host repeats.

For the hosts who set the table that everyone wants to be seated at — the patrons of a private estate in Aspen, a charity gala on the Upper East Side, a wedding of two old families on the coast of Maine — the question is never the band. The question is the tone. Who walks into the room, and who walks out of it talking?

Chris Sullivan has spent a decade answering that question quietly. Blues, soul, and the kind of storytelling that doesn't require a setlist so much as a sense of when to lean in and when to step back. Two programs on the bill, one voice carrying both. No costume piece. No karaoke. No press-play-and-pray.

What follows is, in effect, an introduction by the house. The Repertoire, a few favorite rooms, and — for the discreet inquirer — a private line.

— The Editors · Sullivan Fantastic

A milestone celebration, on a private floor, with a guest list of family by blood and by choice. The room held — through the toast, the ballad, the dance — long after the official program had ended. The job, on those nights, is restraint.
