— Act IV · The Host —

A man.
A microphone.
A full house.

Chris Sullivan is a singer, storyteller, and live act — equally at home on a listening-room stage and inside a black-tie ballroom.

Trained on blues and soul and raised on theatre, he reads a room the way a conductor reads a score — adjusting tempo, register, and silence in real time. There are no backing tracks, no costume pieces, no press-play-and-pray. Just a voice that moves, a band that follows it, and an evening guests will still be talking about over coffee the next morning.

Based in the United States. Touring worldwide on request. Inquiries route directly to Chris; replies on serious rooms inside 48 hours.

V · The Three ActsHow an evening is built.

From inquiry → curtain
I.
— Act One —

We Talk.

One call. Twenty minutes. The room, the guests, the moment you're building toward. We listen for the things that don't make it onto a rider — the first dance, the donor's favorite ballad, the silence before the toast. The brief is yours.

II.
— Act Two —

We Design.

A bespoke setlist, a configuration that fits your space (solo, trio, full seven-piece), and a technical plot built around your venue — not a generic one stapled to it. You see the program before it's locked. Nothing is a surprise but the music.

III.
— Act Three —

The Curtain Rises.

We arrive early, soundcheck quietly, and step on at the moment you asked us to. Then we read the house and respond — accelerating, lingering, holding the silences. You host. We carry the room. The guests leave talking.
