# Direction A — Black-Tie Cinematic

This mockup positions Sullivan Fantastic as a Bond-opening-sequence whisper: pure black, champagne gold used scarcely so it reads as precious, oversized italic Cormorant Garamond display set against full-bleed darkened imagery with slow ken-burns and fade-in reveals — no bouncy motion, no shouting CTAs, no pricing. The headline ("An evening the room will remember.") and the single quiet "Inquire" link in the nav signal that this is an *invitation*, not a transaction. The ideal client is the host who already knows what tier they're shopping in: ultra-high-net-worth event chairs, foundation directors, family-office estate managers, and luxury destination wedding planners booking black-tie galas, private estate evenings, milestone birthdays, and yachts where the question isn't "how much" but "is he available." Everything about the page — the hairline gold rules between services, the massive italic pull-quote, the "by introduction" register, the placeholder concierge email instead of a phone tree — is engineered to make that client feel like they've found a closed door with their name already on the list.
